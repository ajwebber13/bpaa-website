Biography

Brother Lester J. McKee is a Fall 1975 initiate of the Beta Phi Chapter of Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc. He began his 40-year career in education in 1985 at Dillard University, where he taught biology and chemistry and supported first-year science majors through a National Institutes of Health-funded minority research program.

He later served at William Paterson University, rising to Director of Minority Education, where he supported minority, low-income, sponsored, and non-traditional students through academic and leadership development programs.

At Newark Public Schools, Brother McKee became the Founding Director of the Office of School-to-Careers and College Initiatives, leading career and technical education programs and managing federal Perkins grant funding in partnership with major corporations including Ford, Lucent Technologies, Bell Atlantic, and Porsche.

He continued his leadership in Atlanta Public Schools, serving in senior administrative roles focused on curriculum, accountability, research, and college readiness. He was instrumental in developing Georgia’s first Early College High School in partnership with Georgia State University.

In 2012, he founded a consulting firm supporting educational transformation across the United States. His work includes the development of Norfolk Public Schools’ Open Campus High School and continued leadership in nonprofit and healthcare organizational development. He later served in executive roles with the College Success Foundation, where he redesigned student support systems and improved national student success outcomes.
