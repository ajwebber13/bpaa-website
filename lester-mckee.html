Brother Lester McKee F'75

Lester McKee began his 40-year career in 1985 at Dillard University, teaching and supporting first-year science majors. He taught biology and chemistry and administered a National Institutes of Health-funded minority research program. He later served at William Paterson University, advancing to Director of Minority Education, supporting minority, low-income, and non-traditional students. At Newark Public Schools, he became Founding Director of the Office of School-to-Careers and College Initiatives, leading career and technical education programs and managing federal grant funding in partnership with major corporations including Lucent Technologies, Ford Motor Company, Bell Atlantic, and Porsche. He later served in Atlanta Public Schools in senior leadership roles, overseeing curriculum, research, accountability, and college readiness initiatives. He helped design Georgia’s first Early College High School in partnership with Georgia State University. In 2012, he founded a consulting firm supporting education and organizational development across the United States. His work includes developing Norfolk Public Schools’ Open Campus High School and supporting healthcare and nonprofit institutions. He later served as Director of Programs and Executive Director roles with the College Success Foundation, where he redesigned student support systems, improved outcomes, and expanded national program impact..
