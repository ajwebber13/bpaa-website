Lester J. McKee, Jr.

Board Member / Golden Brother

Lester McKee is a 1975 initiate of the Beta Phi Chapter of Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Inc. He began his 40-year career in 1985 at Dillard University, teaching biology and chemistry and supporting first-year science majors.

He later served at William Paterson University as Director of Minority Education supporting underrepresented students.

At Newark Public Schools, he founded the Office of School-to-Careers and College Initiatives, partnering with Fortune 500 companies.

He later served in Atlanta Public Schools in senior leadership roles and helped design Georgia’s first Early College High School.

In 2012, he founded a consulting firm supporting education, healthcare, and nonprofit organizations nationwide.
